The ice age certainly did Bordeaux some favors, leaving behind an environment that can only be described as a wine-growing paradise. Bordeaux thrives with limestone and calcium. In fact, Bordeaux's success can largely be linked to the incredible soil structure it sits on. A variety of clay (hardpan), limestone, gravel and siliceous soil makes up this diverse yet geographically perfect landscape. With two major rivers (Garonne and Dordogne) running through the center of Bordeaux and an array of tributaries from the Gironde Estuary, Bordeaux is home to an incredible watershed with ample groundwater, perfect for winemaking.
